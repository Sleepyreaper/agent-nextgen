📖 How to Read This Report

Correlation (r) measures how well AI and human scores move together. r=1.0 is perfect agreement, r=0 is random, r<0 is inverted. For rubric scoring, r≥0.7 is strong, r≥0.5 is moderate, r<0.3 is weak.

MAE (Mean Absolute Error) for same-scale comparisons (Milo estimates) — how far off the AI is on average. MAE<0.5 on a 0-3 scale is excellent, MAE<1.0 is acceptable.

Selection Accuracy — what percentage of students would the AI classify the same way as faculty (selected vs. not). Above 80% is the target.

Divergent cases — where AI and human scores disagree most — are the highest-value learning opportunities. Every divergence should be investigated: is the AI wrong, or did it see something the human missed?

⚠️ If most records show "Awaiting AI" scores, you need to reprocess training data through the current pipeline first.
