🧪 Milo Model Validation

What does this do? Validation scores every training student through Milo's current model and compares the AI's prediction against the known outcome (Selected vs Not Selected). This reveals:
  • Accuracy — how often the model's prediction matches reality
  • Precision / Recall / F1 — how well it identifies truly selected students
  • Score separation — gap between accepted and not-selected average scores
  • Per-student breakdown — which students the model gets right or wrong and why

Students scoring ≥ threshold are predicted "Selected". Students with missing application data will score 0.

💡 About Training Data

Training data helps the system understand what makes excellent applicants by learning from historical examples. The overall score shown is the AI's evaluation of each historical student.

These applicants are typically top of their class (though not always). Every training example runs through the full, rigorous evaluation pipeline so the final recommendation reflects a complete perspective.

  • 🧙 Comprehensive evaluation compares new applicants against historical excellence
  • 🌊 School context learns patterns from schools that produced successful students
  • 💇 Transcript analysis calibrates grade expectations based on past selections
  • 👸 Application review identifies essay qualities that correlate with success
  • 🗡️ Recommendation analysis weights recommendation attributes from past winners

🔄 Reprocess Training Documents

What this does: Re-downloads every PDF/document from storage for each training student, re-runs the text extraction and Belle section detection pipeline, and updates the application_text, transcript_text, and recommendation_text fields in the database.
